Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked pasta dish made of stacked layers of flat pasta with fillings like ragù (meat and tomato sauce), béchamel, vegetables, and cheeses such as ricotta, mozzarella, and parmesan, seasoned with herbs like basil, oregano, and garlic. The dish is assembled in layers and baked until bubbly, with variations using fresh or no-boil noodles, and different sauces.

Preparation Steps

1. Make the sauce: Brown ground meat (beef, sausage) with onions and garlic, then simmer with tomato sauce and seasonings.

2. Prepare the pasta: Cook lasagna noodles according to package directions (or use no-boil/fresh).

3. Mix cheeses: Combine ricotta with herbs and other cheeses in a separate bowl.

4. Assemble layers: In a baking dish, layer sauce, noodles, cheese mixture, and more sauce, repeating until the dish is full.

5. Top and bake: Finish with a final layer of sauce and mozzarella/parmesan, then bake at around 350-375°F (175-190°C) until hot, bubbly, and golden brown.

Enjoy!
